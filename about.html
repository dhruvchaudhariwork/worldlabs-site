The e-commerce story

I launched my first Shopify brand in October 2025. Within five months it scaled to ~$175K MRR. The second brand launched shortly after and hit ~$10K MRR in its first month. Both run on the same automation stack — AI agents handling creative, customer support, and operations on top of Shopify.

The playbook is simple: layer AI agents on top of Shopify for everything from CX to inventory. That means I don't need to hire a support team, a VA for dispute management, or someone to watch inventory levels. The agents do it. I approve or override from my phone.

Both brands sit on the same infrastructure, which means I can spin up new verticals quickly without adding headcount. That's the thesis behind Optonomous — if this stack works for my brands, it works for anyone's.

Why Optonomous

Every e-commerce brand that crosses $100K/mo GMV hits the same bottleneck: ops busywork eats the team alive. You're juggling a helpdesk, a chargeback tool, a subscription platform, inventory spreadsheets, and a VA — spending $4-5K/mo on tools that don't talk to each other and still require your time.

I know because I was that person. I was running two brands, managing support tickets at midnight, manually filing dispute evidence, and checking inventory levels in a spreadsheet. The tools existed, but none of them worked together, and none of them were autonomous.

So I built the stack I wanted. AI agents that actually run your ops — not tools that give you more dashboards to check. Optonomous is the productized version of what already powers my own brands.
